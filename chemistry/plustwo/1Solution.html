Question 1

  1. Define Molarity

  2. Calculate the molarity of a solution containing 10 g of NaOH in 450 ml solution.

Answer

  1. \(M=\frac{n}{v}\)
    Number of moles of solute per unit volume of solution
  2. $$\begin{align} M&=\frac{n}{v}\\[6pt] M&=\frac{W_B\times 1000}{M_B\times V}\\[6pt] M&=\frac{10\times 1000}{40\times450}\\[6pt] &=\frac{100}{45\times 4}\\[6pt] &=\underline{\underline{0.55\, \mathrm{M}}} \end{align}$$

Question 2

A solution is made by weighing 10 g of NaCl and adding 40 g of water. This solution is .........

  1. 10% (w/w)
  2. 20% (w/w)
  3. 25% (w/w)
  4. 5% (w/w)

Answer

$$\begin{align} \text{mass%}&=\frac{\text{Mass of one comp.}}{\text{Total mass}}\times 100\\[6pt] &=\frac{10}{40+10}\times100\\[6pt] &=\underline{\underline{20\%}} \end{align}$$
