Question 1

If \( f: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \) is given by \( f(x) = \cos(x) \) and \( g: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \) is given by \( g(x) = 3x^2 \), then find \( g \circ f \) and \( f \circ g \).

Answer

\( f(x) = \cos(x) \)
\( g(x) = 3x^2 \)
1. For \( g \circ f \): \begin{align} (g \circ f)(x) &= g(f(x)) \\&= g(\cos(x)) \\&= 3(\cos(x))^2 \\&= 3\cos^2(x) \end{align} 2. For \( f \circ g \): \begin{align} (f \circ g)(x) &= f(g(x)) \\&= f(3x^2) \\&= \cos(3x^2) \end{align}

Question 2

Construct a \(2 \times 3\) matrix whose elements are given by \(a_{ij} = 2i + j\).

Answer

\[ \begin{aligned} &a_{11} = 2(1) + 1 = 3 \\ &a_{12} = 2(1) + 2 = 4 \\ &a_{13} = 2(1) + 3 = 5 \\ &a_{21} = 2(2) + 1 = 5 \\ &a_{22} = 2(2) + 2 = 6 \\ &a_{23} = 2(2) + 3 = 7 \\ \end{aligned} \] Thus, the matrix is: \[ \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 4 & 5 \\ 5 & 6 & 7 \\ \end{bmatrix} \]

Question 3

(i) Let \( A \) be a square matrix of order \( 3 \times 3 \), then \( \left\vert k A \right\vert =\) _______.
A. \(\, k \left| A \right|\)
B. \(\, k^2 \left| A \right|\)
C. \(\, k^3 \left| A \right|\)
D. \(\, 3k \left| A \right|\)
(ii) Find the value of \( x \) for which \( \left| \begin{array}{c} 3 & x \\ x & 1 \end{array} \right| = \left| \begin{array}{cc} 3 & 2 \\ 4 & 1 \end{array} \right|\)

Answer

(i) Given that \( A \) is a \( 3 \times 3 \) square matrix, we need to find the determinant of the matrix \( kA \). Recall that for any \( n \times n \) matrix \( A \) and scalar \( k \), the determinant of \( kA \) is given by: \[ \left| kA \right| = k^n \left| A \right| \] In this case, \( n = 3 \): \[ \left| kA \right| = k^3 \left| A \right| \] Therefore, the answer is: \[ \left| kA \right| = k^3 \left| A \right| \] (ii) ### Step 1: Determinant of the left-hand side matrix \[ \left| \begin{array}{cc} 3 & x \\ x & 1 \end{array} \right| = 3 \cdot 1 - x \cdot x = 3 - x^2 \] ### Step 2: Determinant of the right-hand side matrix \[ \left| \begin{array}{cc} 3 & 2 \\ 4 & 1 \end{array} \right| = 3 \cdot 1 - 2 \cdot 4 = 3 - 8 = -5 \] ### Step 3: Set the determinants equal and solve for \( x \) \[ 3 - x^2 = -5 \] \[ -x^2 = -5 - 3 \] \[ -x^2 = -8 \] \[ x^2 = 8 \] \[ x = \pm \sqrt{8} = \pm 2\sqrt{2} \] Thus, the value of \( x \) is \( x = \pm 2\sqrt{2} \).

Question 4

Find the value of \( k \) so that the function \[ f(x) = \begin{cases} kx + 1, & \text{if } x \leq 5 \\ 3x - 5, & \text{if } x > 5 \end{cases} \] is continuous.

Answer

### Step 1: Continuity condition at \( x = 5 \) For \( f(x) \) to be continuous at \( x = 5 \), the left-hand limit and the right-hand limit at that point must be equal: #### Left-hand limit (\( x \to 5^- \)): \[ \lim_{x \to 5^-} f(x) = k(5) + 1 = 5k + 1 \] #### Right-hand limit (\( x \to 5^+ \)): \[ \lim_{x \to 5^+} f(x) = 3(5) - 5 = 15 - 5 = 10 \] ### Step 2: Set the limits equal Set the left-hand and right-hand limits equal: \[ 5k + 1 = 10 \] ### Step 3: Solve for \( k \) \[ 5k = 10 - 1 \] \[ 5k = 9 \] \[ k = \frac{9}{5} \] Thus, the value of \( k \) is \( k = \frac{9}{5} \).

Question 5

(i) \(\frac{d}{dx} \sqrt{\sin x} =\) _______.

  1. \(\sqrt{\cos x}\)
  2. \(2 \sqrt{\sin x}\)
  3. \(\frac{\cos x}{2 \sqrt{\sin x}}\)
  4. \(\frac{\sin x}{2 \sqrt{\cos x}}\)

(ii) Find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) if \(y + \sin y = \cos x\).

Answer

(i) To find \(\frac{d}{dx} \sqrt{\sin x}\), we use the chain rule: Let \(y = \sqrt{\sin x} = (\sin x)^{1/2}\). Using the chain rule: \[ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{2} (\sin x)^{-1/2} \cdot \cos x = \frac{\cos x}{2 \sqrt{\sin x}} \] So, the answer is: **(C)** \(\frac{\cos x}{2 \sqrt{\sin x}}\)
(ii) To find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\), differentiate both sides with respect to \(x\):
1. Differentiate the left side: \[ \frac{d}{dx}(y + \sin y) = \frac{dy}{dx} + \cos y \cdot \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{dx}(1 + \cos y) \] 2. Differentiate the right side: \[ \frac{d}{dx}(\cos x) = -\sin x \] Putting it all together: \[ \frac{dy}{dx}(1 + \cos y) = -\sin x \] Now, solve for \(\frac{dy}{dx}\): \[ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{-\sin x}{1 + \cos y} \] Thus, the final answer is: \[ \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{-\sin x}{1 + \cos y} \]

Question 6

Let \( f(x) = x^2 - 4x + 6 \).
(i) Find \( f'(x) \).
(ii) Find the interval in which \( f \) is increasing.

Answer

(i) To find the derivative of \( f(x) \), we differentiate each term: \[ f'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}(x^2) - \frac{d}{dx}(4x) + \frac{d}{dx}(6) \] Calculating the derivatives, we get: \[ f'(x) = 2x - 4 \] (ii) The function \( f(x) \) is increasing where its derivative \( f'(x) \) is positive: \[ f'(x) > 0 \] Setting up the inequality: \[ 2x - 4 > 0 \] Solving for \( x \): \[ 2x > 4 \implies x > 2 \] Thus, the function \( f(x) \) is increasing in the interval: \[ (2, \infty) \] ### Summary of Results - **(i)** \( f'(x) = 2x - 4 \) - **(ii)** The function \( f \) is increasing on the interval \( (2, \infty) \).

Question 7

(i) Order of the differential equation \[ \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} + x\left(\frac{dy}{dx}\right)^3 - y = 0 \] (ii) Find the general solution of the differential equation \[ \frac{dy}{dx} = (1 + x^2)(1 + y^2) \]

Answer

(i)**To determine the order of the differential equation**, we identify the highest derivative present in the equation.
- The highest derivative is \(\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}\).
Thus, the order of the differential equation is **2**.
The correct answer is:
**(B)** 2
(ii)This is a separable differential equation, and we can rewrite it as: \[ \frac{dy}{1 + y^2} = (1 + x^2) \, dx \] Now, integrate both sides:
1. **Integrating the left side:** \[ \int \frac{dy}{1 + y^2} = \tan^{-1}(y) + C_1 \] 2. **Integrating the right side:** \[ \int (1 + x^2) \, dx = x + \frac{x^3}{3} + C_2 \] Combining the results, we get: \[ \tan^{-1}(y) = x + \frac{x^3}{3} + C \] where \( C = C_2 - C_1 \) is a constant of integration. Thus, the general solution of the differential equation is: \[ \tan^{-1}(y) = x + \frac{x^3}{3} + C. \]
