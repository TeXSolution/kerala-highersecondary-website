Chapter 1 Arithmetic Sequence

Question 1

Consider the arithmetic sequence \(1, 7, 13, 19, \ldots\)
a) What is the common difference?

Answer

$$\begin{align} d&=13-7\\ &=\underline{\underline{6}} \end{align}$$

Question 2

What is the 25th term of the arithmetic sequence \(1,11,21,\ldots\)?

Answer

$$\begin{align} x_1&=1\\ d&=11-1\\ &=\underline{\underline{10}}\\ \underbrace{x_{25}=x_1}_{+24}&+24d\\ &=1+(24\times 10)\\ &=1+240\\ x_{25}&=\underline{\underline{241}} \end{align}$$

Question 3

10th and 12th terms of an arithmetic sequence are 32 and 46.
a) What is the common difference?
b) What is the first term of this sequence?

Answer

$$\begin{align} x_{10}&=32\\ x_{12}&=46\\ \end{align}$$

a) $$\begin{align} x_{12}&=x_{10}+2d\\ 46&=32+2d\\ 46-32&=2d\\ 14&=2d\\ d&=14/2\\ d&=\underline{\underline{7}} \end{align}$$

b) $$\begin{align} x_{1}&=x_{10}-9d\\ &=32-(9\times7)\\ &=32-63\\ x_1&=\underline{\underline{-31}} \end{align}$$

Question 4

Write three arithmetic sequences with the sum of the first 7 terms as 70.

Answer

$$\begin{align} m&=>x_4\\ S_7&=m\times 7\\ x_4&=\frac{70}{7}\\ x_4&=10 \end{align}$$

\(7,8,9,{\color{yellow}{10}},11,12,13\quad\) \(\rightarrow\color{red}{70}\)

\(4,6,8,{\color{yellow}{10}},12,14,16\quad\) \(\rightarrow\color{red}{70}\)

\(19,16,13,{\color{yellow}{10}},7,4,1\quad\) \(\rightarrow\color{red}{70}\)

Question 5

The sum of the first 3 terms of an arithmetic sequence is 30 and the sum of the first 7 terms is 140.
(i) What is the 2nd term of the sequence?
(ii) What is the 4th term of the sequence?
(iii) What are the first three terms of the sequence?

Answer

(i) $$\begin{align} x_2&=\frac{30}{3}\\ &=\underline{\underline{10}} \end{align}$$

(ii) $$\begin{align} x_4&=\frac{140}{7}\\ &=\underline{\underline{20}} \end{align}$$

(iii) $$\begin{align} b&=\frac{a+c}{2}\\ x_3&=\frac{x_2+x_4}{2}\\ &=\frac{10+20}{2}\\ &=15\\ d&=15-10\\ d&=5\\ x_1&=10-5 \end{align}$$ \(\underline{\underline{5,10,15}}\)

Question 6

The sum of the 11th and 21st term of an arithmetic sequence is 80. What is the 16th term?

Answer

\(x_{16}=>m\)
\(x_{16}=\frac{80}{2}=\underline{\underline{40}}\)

Question 7

In each of arithmetic sequences below, some of the terms are not written, but indicated by \(\bigcirc\), Find out these numbers:

  1. \(24, 42, \bigcirc, \bigcirc,\ldots\)
  2. \(\bigcirc, 24, 42, \bigcirc,\ldots\)
  3. \(\bigcirc, \bigcirc, 24, 42,\ldots\)
  4. \(24, \bigcirc, 42, \bigcirc,\ldots\)
  5. \(\bigcirc, 24, \bigcirc, 42,\ldots\)
  6. \(24, \bigcirc, \bigcirc, 42,\ldots\)

Answer

  1. \(24, 42, \bigcirc, \bigcirc,\ldots\)
    $$\begin{align} d=x_2-x_1\\ d=42-24\\ d=18\\ x_3=x_2+d\\ x_3=42+18\\ x_3=\underline{\underline{60}}\\ x_4=x_3+d\\ x_4=60+18\\ x_4=\underline{\underline{78}} \end{align}$$
    \(24, 42, {\color{yellow}{60}}, {\color{yellow}{78}},\ldots\)

  2. \(\bigcirc, 24, 42, \bigcirc,\ldots\)
    $$\begin{align} d=x_3-x_2\\ d=42-24\\ d=18\\ x_4=x_3+d\\ x_4=42+18\\ x_4=\underline{\underline{60}}\\ x_1=x_2-d\\ x_1=24-18\\ x_1=\underline{\underline{6}} \end{align}$$
    \({\color{yellow}{6}}, 24, 42, {\color{yellow}{60}},\ldots\)

  3. \(\bigcirc, \bigcirc, 24, 42,\ldots\)
    $$\begin{align} d=x_4-x_3\\ d=42-24\\ d=18\\ x_2=x_3-d\\ x_2=24-18\\ x_2=\underline{\underline{6}}\\ x_1=x_2-d\\ x_1=6-18\\ x_1=\underline{\underline{-12}} \end{align}$$
    \({\color{yellow}{-12}},{\color{yellow}{6}}, 24, 42,\ldots\)

  4. \(24, \bigcirc, 42, \bigcirc,\ldots\)
    $$\begin{align} x_1+2d=x_3\\ 24+2d=42\\ 2d=42-24\\ 2d=18\\ d=\frac{18}{2}\\ d=9\\ x_2=x_1+d\\ x_2=24+9\\ x_2=\underline{\underline{33}}\\ x_4=x_3+d\\ x_4=42+18\\ x_4=\underline{\underline{60}} \end{align}$$
    \(24, {\color{yellow}{33}},42,{\color{yellow}{51}},\ldots\)

  5. \(\bigcirc, 24, \bigcirc, 42,\ldots\)
    $$\begin{align} x_2+2d=x_4\\ 24+2d=42\\ 2d=42-24\\ 2d=18\\ d=\frac{18}{2}\\ d=9\\ x_3=x_2+d\\ x_3=24+9\\ x_3=\underline{\underline{33}}\\ x_1=x_2-d\\ x_1=24-8\\ x_1=\underline{\underline{15}} \end{align}$$
    \({\color{yellow}{15}},24,{\color{yellow}{33}}, 42,\ldots\)

  6. \(24,\bigcirc, \bigcirc, 42,\ldots\)
    $$\begin{align} x_1+3d=x_4\\ 24+3d=42\\ 3d=42-24\\ 3d=18\\ d=\frac{18}{3}\\ d=6\\ x_2=x_1+d\\ x_2=24+6\\ x_2=\underline{\underline{30}}\\ x_3=x_2+d\\ x_3=30+6\\ x_3=\underline{\underline{36}} \end{align}$$
    \(24,{\color{yellow}{30}},{\color{yellow}{36}}, 42,\ldots\)

Question 8

Which of the following relations on \(\mathrm{A}=\{1,2,3\}\) is an equivalence relation?

  1. \((1,1),(2,2),(3,3)\)
  2. \((1,1),(2,2),(3,3),(1,2)\)
  3. \((1,1),(3,3),(1,3),(3,1)\)
  4. None of these

Answer

\((1,1),(2,2),(3,3)\)

Question 9

Let \(\mathrm{R}\) be a relation defined on \(\mathrm{A}=\{1,2,3\}\) by \(\mathrm{R}=\{(1,3),(3,1),(2,2)\}\). \(\mathrm{R}\) is

  1. Reflexive
  2. Symmetric
  3. Transitive
  4. Reflexive but not transitive

Answer

Symmetric

Question 10

Let \(\mathrm{R}\) be the relation in the set \(\{1,2,3,4\}\) given by \(\mathrm{R}=\{(1,2),(2,2),(1,1),(4,4),(1,3),(3,3),(3,2)\}\). Choose the correct answer.

  1. \(\mathrm{R}\) is reflexive and symmetric but not transitive.
  2. \(\mathrm{R}\) is reflexive and transitive but not symmetric.
  3. \(\mathrm{R}\) is symmetric and transitive but not reflexive.
  4. \(\mathrm{R}\) is an equivalence relation.

Answer

\(\mathrm{R}\) is reflexive and transitive but not symmetric.

Question 11

Consider ther relation \(\mathrm{R}\) in the set \(\mathbb{N}\) of natural numbers difined as \(\mathrm{R}=\{(a,b)\colon ab \text{ is a factor of } 6\}\). Determine whether the relation is reflexive, symmetric or transitive

Answer

\(1,2,3,6\) are factor of 6

$$\begin{align}\mathrm{R}&=\{(1,1),(1,2),(2,1),(1,3),(3,1),\\ &\qquad(6,1),(1,6),(2,3),(3,2)\}\end{align}$$

\(2\in\mathbb{N}\) but \((2,2)\notin\mathrm{R}\qquad\) Not reflexive

\((1,2),(2,1),(1,3),(3,1),(6,1),(1,6),(2,3),(3,2)\qquad\) Symmetric

\((2,3)\in \mathrm{R}\quad\) \((3,2)\in \mathrm{R}\quad\) but \((2,2)\notin \mathrm{R}\qquad\) Not transitive
