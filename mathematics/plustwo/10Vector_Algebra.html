Chapter 10 Vector Algebra

Question 1

(i) \(A(1,5,3)\) and \(B(4,5,7)\) be two points, then find vector \(\overrightarrow{AB}\).
(ii) Find a unit vector in the direction of \(\overrightarrow{AB}\)?
(ii) Which of the following is vector perpendicular to \(\overrightarrow{AB}\)?
\(\quad\)(a) \(15\hat{i} + 20\hat{k}\)
\(\quad\)(b) \(15\hat{i}-20\hat{k}\)
\(\quad\)(c) \(-5\hat{j}\)
\(\quad\)(d) \(5\hat{k}\)

Answer

(i) $$\begin{align} \overrightarrow{AB}&=(4-1)\hat{i}+(5-5)\hat{j}+(7-3)\hat{k}\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{3\hat{i}+0\hat{j}+4\hat{k}}} \end{align}$$

(ii) $$\begin{align} \text{unit vector along \(\overrightarrow{AB}\)}&=\frac{\overrightarrow{AB}}{\left\vert \overrightarrow{AB}\right\vert}\\[4pt] \overrightarrow{AB}&=\frac{3\hat{i}+4\hat{k}}{\sqrt{3^2+4^2}}\\[4pt] &=\frac{3\hat{i}+4\hat{k}}{\sqrt{25}}\\[4pt] &=\frac{3\hat{i}+4\hat{k}}{5}\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{\frac{3}{5}\hat{i}+\frac{4}{5}\hat{k}}} \end{align}$$

(iii) $$\begin{align} \overrightarrow{AB}&=3\hat{i}+0\hat{j}+4\hat{k}\\[4pt] &=(3\hat{i}+0\hat{j}+4\hat{k})\cdot(0\hat{i}-5\hat{j}+0\hat{k})\\[4pt] &=0+0+0\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{0}} \end{align}$$

If \(\vec{a}\, \&\, \vec{b}\) are \(\perp^\text{r}\) then \(\hat{a}\cdot\hat{b}=0\)

\(-5\hat{j}\) is \(\perp^\text{r}\) to \(\overrightarrow{AB}\)

(c) \(\underline{\underline{-5\hat{j}}}\)

Question 2

Consider the vectors \(\vec{a}=\hat{i}+2\hat{j}-5\hat{k}\) and \(\vec{b}=-\hat{i}+7\hat{k}\) find a unit vector in the direction of \(\vec{a}+\vec{b}\)

Answer

$$\begin{align} \vec{a}+\vec{b}&=(1+-1)\hat{i}+(2+0)\hat{j}+(-5+7)\hat{k}\\[4pt] &=0\hat{i}+2\hat{j}+2\hat{k}\\[4pt] \text{unit vector along \(\vec{a}+\vec{b}\)}&=\frac{\vec{a}+\vec{b}}{\left\vert \vec{a}+\vec{b}\right\vert}\\[4pt] &=\frac{2\hat{j}+2\hat{k}}{\sqrt{2^2+2^2}}\\[4pt] &=\frac{2\hat{j}+2\hat{k}}{\sqrt{2\times 4}}\\[4pt] &=\frac{2\hat{j}+2\hat{k}}{2\sqrt{2}}\\[4pt] &=\frac{\cancel{2}\hat{j}}{\cancel{2}\sqrt{2}}+\frac{\cancel{2}\hat{k}}{\cancel{2}\sqrt{2}}\\ &=\underline{\underline{\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\hat{j}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\hat{k}}} \end{align}$$

Question 3

\(\vec{a}=2\hat{i}+3\hat{j}+4\hat{k}\)
\(\vec{b}=\hat{i}+\hat{j}-2\hat{k}\)
(a) Find \(\vec{a}\cdot\vec{b}\)
(a) Find \(\left\vert\vec{a}\right\vert\) and \(\left\vert\vec{b}\right\vert\)

Answer

(a) $$\begin{align} \vec{a}\cdot\vec{b}&=(2\times1)+(3\times1)+(4\times-2)\\[4pt] &=2+3-8\\[4pt] &=5-8\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{-3}} \end{align}$$

(b) $$\begin{align} \left\vert\vec{a}\right\vert&=\sqrt{2^2+3^2+4^2}\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{\sqrt{29}}}\\[4pt] \left\vert\vec{b}\right\vert&=\sqrt{1^2+1^2+(-2)^2}\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{\sqrt{6}}} \end{align}$$

Question 4

The position vectors of three points \(A,B,C\) are given to be \(\hat{i}+3\hat{j}+3\hat{k}, 4\hat{i}+\hat{k}\) and \(-2\hat{i}+4\hat{j}+2\hat{k}\) respectively
(a) Find \(\overrightarrow{AB}\) and \(\overrightarrow{AC}\)
(a) Find the angle between \(\overrightarrow{AB}\) and \(\overrightarrow{AC}\)

Answer

(a) $$\begin{align} \overrightarrow{AB}&=(4-1)\hat{i}+(0-3)\hat{j}+(1-3)\hat{k}\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{3\hat{i}-3\hat{j}-2\hat{k}}}\\[4pt] \overrightarrow{AC}&=(-2-1)\hat{i}+(4-3)\hat{j}+(2-3)\hat{k}\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{-3\hat{i}+\hat{j}-\hat{k}}} \end{align}$$

(b) $$\begin{align} \cos\theta&=\frac{\vec{a}\cdot\vec{b}}{\left\vert \vec{a}\right\vert \left\vert \vec{b}\right\vert}\\[4pt] \overrightarrow{AB}&= 3\hat{i}-3\hat{j}-2\hat{k}\\[4pt] \overrightarrow{AC}&= -3\hat{i}+\hat{j}-\hat{k}\\[4pt] \left\vert\overrightarrow{AB}\right\vert&= \sqrt{3^2+(-3)^2+(-2)^2}\\[4pt] &= \sqrt{9+9+4}\\[4pt] &= \sqrt{22}\\[4pt] \left\vert\overrightarrow{AC}\right\vert&= \sqrt{(-3)^2+1^2+(-1)^2}\\[4pt] &= \sqrt{9+1+1}\\[4pt] &= \sqrt{11}\\[4pt] \overrightarrow{AB}\cdot\overrightarrow{AC}&=(3\times -3)+(-3\times1)+(-2\times-1)\\[4pt] &=-9-3+2\\[4pt] &=-10\\[4pt] \cos\theta&=\frac{\overrightarrow{AB}\cdot\overrightarrow{AC}}{\left\vert\overrightarrow{AB} \right\vert \left\vert \overrightarrow{AC}\right\vert}\\[4pt] &=\frac{-10}{\sqrt{22}\times\sqrt{11}}\\[4pt] \theta&=\cos^{-1}\left(\frac{-10}{\sqrt{22\times11}}\right)\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{\cos^{-1}\left(\frac{-10}{\sqrt{242}}\right)}} \end{align}$$

Question 5

If \(\vec{a}=3\hat{i}-\hat{j}-5\hat{k}\) and \(\vec{b}=\hat{i}-5\hat{j}+3\hat{k}\) show that \(\vec{a}+\vec{b}\) and \(\vec{a}-\vec{b}\) are perpendicular.

Answer

To prove: \((\vec{a}+\vec{b})\cdot(\vec{a}-\vec{b})=0\)
$$\begin{align} \vec{a}+\vec{b}&=(3+1)\hat{i}+(-1-5)\hat{j}+(-5+3)\hat{k}\\[4pt] &=4\hat{i}-6\hat{j}-2\hat{k}\\[4pt] \vec{a}-\vec{b}&=(3-1)\hat{i}+(-1--5)\hat{j}+(-5-3)\hat{k}\\[4pt] &=2\hat{i}+4\hat{j}-8\hat{k}\\[4pt] (\vec{a}+\vec{b})\cdot(\vec{a}-\vec{b})&=(4\times2)+(-6\times4)+(-2\times-8)\\[4pt] &=8+-24+16\\[4pt] &=24-24\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{0}}\\[4pt] \end{align}$$

So \(\vec{a}+\vec{b}\) and \(\vec{a}-\vec{b}\) are perpendicular.
