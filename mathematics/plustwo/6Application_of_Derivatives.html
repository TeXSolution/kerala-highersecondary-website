Chapter 6 Application of Derivatives

Question 1

Find the rate of change of the area of a circle with respect to its radius r where r = 4 cm.

Answer

$$\begin{align} A&=\pi r^2\\[4pt] \frac{dA}{dr}&=\frac{d}{dr}\pi r^2\\[4pt] &=\pi \frac{d}{dr}r^2\\[4pt] &=\pi 2r\\[4pt] \left.\frac{dA}{dr}\right\vert_{r=4cm}&=\pi\times2\times4\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{8\pi\, \mathrm{cm}^2/\mathrm{cm}}} \end{align}$$

Question 2

A sphere has a variable radius. Find the rate at which its volume is increasing with the radius when the radius is 10 cm.

Answer

$$\begin{align} V&=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\\[4pt] \frac{dv}{dr}&=\frac{4}{\cancel{3}}\pi\times \cancel{3}r^2\\[4pt] &=4\pi r^2\\[4pt] \left.\frac{dV}{dr}\right\vert_{r=10cm}&=4\times\pi\times10^2\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{400\pi\, \mathrm{cm}^3/\mathrm{cm}}} \end{align}$$

Question 3

The radius of a circle is increasing uniformly at the rate of 6 cm/s. Find the rate at which the area of the circle is increasing when the radius is 10 cm.

Answer

$$\begin{align} \frac{dr}{dt}&=+6\,\mathrm{cm}/\mathrm{s}\\[4pt] A&=\pi r^2\\[4pt] \frac{dA}{dt}&=\frac{d}{dt}\pi r^2\\[4pt] &=\pi\frac{d}{dt} r^2\\[4pt] &=\pi2r\frac{dr}{dt}\\[4pt] &=2\pi r\times 6\\[4pt] &=12\pi r\\[4pt] \left.\frac{dA}{dt}\right\vert_{r=10cm}&=12\pi \times 10\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{120\pi\,\mathrm{cm}^2/\mathrm{s}}} \end{align}$$

Question 4

An edge of a cube is increasing at the rate of 4 cm/s. How fast is the volume increasing when the edge is 10 cm?

Answer

$$\begin{align} \frac{dx}{dt}&=+4\,\mathrm{cm}/\mathrm{s}\\[4pt] V&=x^3\\[4pt] \frac{dV}{dt}&=\frac{d}{dt} x^3\\[4pt] &=3x^2\frac{dx}{dt}\\[4pt] &=3x^2\times 4\\[4pt] &=12x^2\\[4pt] \left.\frac{dV}{dt}\right\vert_{x=10cm}&=12\times 10^2\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{1200\,\mathrm{cm}^3/\mathrm{s}}} \end{align}$$

Question 5

A stone is dropped into a quiet lake and waves move in circles at a speed of 4 cm/second. At the instant when radius of the circular wave is 10 cm, how fast is the enclosed area increasing?

Answer

$$\begin{align} \frac{dr}{dt}&=+4\,\mathrm{cm}/\mathrm{s}\\[4pt] A&=\pi r^2\\[4pt] \frac{dA}{dt}&=\frac{d}{dt} \pi r^2\\[4pt] &=\pi \frac{d}{dt}r^2\\[4pt] &=\pi\times 2r\times \frac{dr}{dt}\\[4pt] &=2\pi r\times 4\\[4pt] &=8\pi r\\[4pt] \left.\frac{dA}{dt}\right\vert_{r=10cm}&=8\pi\times 10\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{80\pi\,\mathrm{cm}^2/\mathrm{s}}} \end{align}$$

Question 6

The length of a rectangle is decreasing at the rate of 5 cm/s and the width is increasing at the rate of 4 cm/s. When length is 8 cm and width is 6 cm, find the rate of change of its area.

Answer

$$\begin{align} \frac{dx}{dt}&=-5\,\mathrm{cm}/\mathrm{s}\\[4pt] \frac{dy}{dt}&=+4\,\mathrm{cm}/\mathrm{s}\\[4pt] A&=xy\\[4pt] \frac{dA}{dt}&=x\frac{dy}{dt}+y\frac{dx}{dt}\\[4pt] &=x\times 4+y\times -5\\[4pt] &=4x-5y\\[4pt] \left.\frac{dA}{dt}\right\vert_{x=8,y=6}&=4\times8-5\times6\\[4pt] &=32-30\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{2\,\mathrm{cm}^2/\mathrm{s}}} \end{align}$$

Question 7

Show that the function \(f(x)=3x+17\) is strictly increasing on R.

Answer

$$\begin{align} f(x)&=3x+17\\[4pt] f'(x)&=3\times1+0\\[4pt] &=3\\[4pt] &>0\\[4pt] \therefore& \text{ \(f(x)\) is strictly increasing on R} \end{align}$$

Question 8

Show that the function \(f(x)=e^{2x}\) is strictly increasing on R.

Answer

$$\begin{align} f(x)&=e^{2x}\\[4pt] f'(x)&=2e^{2x}\\[4pt] &=2\left(e^{x}\right)^2\\[4pt] &>0\\[4pt] \therefore& \text{ \(f(x)\) is strictly increasing on R} \end{align}$$

Question 9

Show that the function \(f(x)=x^3-3x^2+3x-100\) is strictly increasing on R.

Answer

$$\begin{align} f(x)&=x^3-3x^2+3x-100\\[4pt] f'(x)&=3x^2-3\times2x+3\times1-0\\[4pt] &=3x^2-6x+3\\[4pt] &=3\left(x^2-2x+1\right)\\[4pt] &=3\left(x-1\right)^2\\[4pt] &>0\\[4pt] \therefore& \text{ \(f(x)\) is strictly increasing on R} \end{align}$$

Question 10

Find the points of local minima and minimum values of the function \(f\) give by \(f(x)=9x^2+12x+2\).

Answer

$$\begin{align} f(x)&=9x^2+12x+2\\[4pt] f'(x)&=9\times2x+12\times1+0\\[4pt] &=18x+12\\[4pt] f'(x)&=0\\[4pt] 18x+12&=0\\[4pt] 18x&=-12\\[4pt] x&=\frac{-12}{18}\\[4pt] x&=\frac{-2}{3}\\[4pt] f''(x)&=18\\[4pt] &>0\\[4pt] x&=\underline{\underline{\frac{-2}{3}}}\quad \text{ is a point of local minima}\\[4pt] f\left(\frac{-2}{3}\right)&=9x^2+12x+2\\[4pt] &=\cancel{9}\times\frac{4}{\cancel{9}}+12\times\frac{-2}{3}+2\\[4pt] &=4+4\times-2+2\\[4pt] &=\underline{\underline{-2}}\quad \text{ Min. Value} \end{align}$$
