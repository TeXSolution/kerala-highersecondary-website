Chapter 3 Matrices

Question 1

\(\mathrm{A}=\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1\\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\) show that \(\mathrm{A}^2-5\mathrm{A}+7\mathrm{I}=0\)
(Where \(\mathrm{I}\) is the identity matrix)

Answer

$$\begin{align} \mathrm{A}^2&=\mathrm{A}\times \mathrm{A}\\[6pt] &=\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1\\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\times \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1\\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] &=\begin{bmatrix} 9-1 & 3+2\\ -3-2 & -1+4 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] &=\begin{bmatrix} 8 & 5\\ -5 & 3 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] 5\mathrm{A}&=5\times\begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1\\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] &=\begin{bmatrix} 15 & 5\\ -5 & 10 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] \mathrm{I}&=\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] 7\mathrm{I}&=\begin{bmatrix} 7 & 0\\ 0 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] \mathrm{A}-5\mathrm{A}+7\mathrm{I}&=\begin{bmatrix} 8 & 5\\ -5 & 3 \end{bmatrix} -\begin{bmatrix} 15 & 5\\ -5 & 10 \end{bmatrix} +\begin{bmatrix} 7 & 0\\ 0 & 7 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] &=\begin{bmatrix} 8-15+7 & 5-5+0\\ -5-{-5}+0 & 3-10+7 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] &=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0\\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] \mathrm{A}-5\mathrm{A}+7\mathrm{I}&=\underline{\underline{0}} \end{align}$$

Question 2

Express the matrix \(\mathrm{A}=\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -2 & -4\\ -1 & 3 & 4\\ 1 & -2 & -3 \end{bmatrix}\) as the sum of a symmetric and skew symmetric matrix.

Answer

\(\mathrm{A}=\frac{1}{2}(\mathrm{A}+\mathrm{A}')+\frac{1}{2}(\mathrm{A}-\mathrm{A}')\)

$$\begin{align}\mathrm{A}&=\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -2 & -4\\ -1 & 3 & 4\\ 1 & -2 & -3 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] \mathrm{A}'&=\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 & 1\\ -2 & 3 & -2\\ -4 & 4 & -3 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] \mathrm{A}+\mathrm{A}'&=\begin{bmatrix} 4 & -3 & -3\\ -3 & 6 & 2\\ -3 & 2 & -6 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] \mathrm{A}-\mathrm{A}'&=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 & -5\\ 1 & 0 & 6\\ 5 & -6 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] \mathrm{A}&=\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{bmatrix} 4 & -3 & -3\\ -3 & 6 & 2\\ -3 & 2 & -6 \end{bmatrix}\right)+\frac{1}{2}\left(\begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 & -5\\ 1 & 0 & 6\\ 5 & -6 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\right)\\[6pt] \mathrm{A}&=\begin{bmatrix} 2 & \frac{-3}{2} & \frac{-3}{2}\\ \frac{-3}{2} & 3 & 1\\ \frac{-3}{2} & 1 & -3 \end{bmatrix}+\begin{bmatrix} 0 & \frac{-1}{2} & \frac{-5}{2}\\ \frac{1}{2} & 0 & 3\\ \frac{5}{2} & -3 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\\[6pt] \mathrm{A}&=\begin{bmatrix} 2 & -2 & -4\\ -1 & 3 & 4\\ 1 & -2 & -3 \end{bmatrix} \end{align}$$
